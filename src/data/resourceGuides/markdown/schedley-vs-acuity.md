## What Acuity Does Well
Before we highlight the gaps, it's worth acknowledging that Acuity is a strong product in its target market. For service businesses, Acuity delivers:
- Payment collection: clients can prepay or leave deposits via Stripe, Square, or PayPal at booking
- Customizable intake forms: collect detailed client info, health history, or business context before the appointment
- HIPAA compliance: available on the Premium plan for healthcare providers
- Package and subscription sales: sell blocks of sessions or memberships directly through the booking page
- Multi-location support: manage bookings across different physical locations
- Deep Squarespace integration: ideal for businesses already on the Squarespace website platform

For a hair salon, therapy practice, or personal trainer, Acuity is a compelling all-in-one platform. The issue is that for the vast majority of knowledge workers, consultants, and sales professionals, these features are irrelevant — and the features they actually need (spam filtering, email validation, qualified booking) are completely absent.

## The Missing Layer: Booking Quality Control
### Acuity Has No Spam or Fraud Protection
Acuity's booking pages are open by default: anyone with your link can book a time. The platform offers no native mechanism to:
- Detect or block spam bookings from bots
- Validate that an email address is real and active before confirming a booking
- Identify and reject unqualified or fraudulent booking attempts
- Filter bookings based on domain, company, or contact quality

For professionals who share their booking link on a website, LinkedIn profile, or email signature, this means every bot, competitor, and time-waster gets equal access to your calendar. The burden of cleanup falls entirely on you after the fact.
### Schedley Solves This at the Source
Schedley's differentiating architecture applies qualification and validation before a booking is confirmed:
- Real-time email validation: checks whether the submitted email is legitimate and active, not just whether the person has inbox access
- Spam pattern detection: identifies suspicious booking behavior and blocks it automatically
- Qualified meeting focus: your calendar receives only meetings from real, verified contacts
- Calendar clutter elimination: no post-hoc cleanup required — your schedule is clean by default

## User Experience & Setup
### Acuity: Power at the Cost of Complexity
Acuity's setup is extensive. The platform requires meaningful configuration to get right — availability rules, intake form logic, payment settings, service definitions, and reminder sequences all need to be configured before your first booking page goes live. Users and reviewers consistently describe this as a 30-minute minimum investment, and some report needing freelancer help for complex configurations.
The interface has been described by reviewers as 'dated' and 'not intuitive,' leading to client-side confusion during booking. The platform was built for feature depth, not user experience simplicity.
### Schedley: Protection in Minutes
Schedley's design philosophy prioritizes fast time-to-value. Connecting your calendar, enabling email validation, and sharing your first protected booking link takes under 10 minutes. There are no complex configuration trees — the system is smart by default.
For professionals who want protection without a steep learning curve, the contrast is significant.
